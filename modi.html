    “Be the change that you wish to see in the world.” - Mahatma Gandhi

    Welcome fellow countrymen! You have the distinguished opportunity to serve on the cabinet of Narendra Modi. This opportunity will come with many grueling challenges, but these must be surmounted in order to help grow our country. We are tasked with the defense of our country, the protection of its people and the empowerment of its economy. This is no easy responsibility, but that is why we have brought together the best and brightest our nation has to offer. We face some of the greatest challenges of our country’s time, with rising tensions with foreign powers and potentially stagnating economic growth, it is up to us to carve out a path towards Indian power on the world stage and bring this great, proud nation to its rightful stature.

    Each of you have a specific role within the government, however in order to work as a cohesive unit, decisions in this cabinet will be based on consensus rather than individual powers. Some of the major issues we must tackle are national security, corruption, economic empowerment and social problems. Come forward with the experience and research expected of you, as one entrusted with the trust and hopes of over one billion people, and there is no doubt that our great nation will prosper. Good luck with your preparation and come ready to hit the ground running!

    Your director will be Jenniston Francis. Jenniston is a senior at EBHS and has been a part of the MUN program throughout high school. Jenniston has been to many different conferences, such as RUMUN, RMC, NAIMUN and NHSMUN. In addition to MUN, he is a part of the local Youth Council, the Future Business Leaders of America club and the Academic Team. He plans to pursue a major in either business or economics in college. Jenniston can’t wait to chair this committee and hopes to make it dynamic and exciting!

    Eric is a senior who has been involved in EB's MUN program, serving in a total of 7 senate, general assembly, and advanced committees at RMC, RUMUN, NAIMUN and NHSMUN. He participates in many other activities during the school year, pursuing his interest both in IR and the sciences. Eric is excited to be your assistant director!

    Mitali Parikh is a junior who has been involved with EBMUN, participating in conferences ranging from senate and economic/ social council committees to advanced committees at RUMUN, RMC, and NHSMUN. Outside of Model UN, she enjoys doing photography, playing piano, and volunteering in many parts of the world. Mitali looks forward to being the assistant director for the 2015 Indian Cabinet committee!
