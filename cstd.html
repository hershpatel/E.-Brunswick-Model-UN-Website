    The CSTD is a fairly new committee in the UN. It was established in 1992 in order to create recommendations based on technology and development. The CSTD has addressed gender inequality, the Millennium Development Goals, and various other themes that help guide legislation. Our CSTD has likewise established a theme that we hope will provoke engaging discussion and heated debate: the Digital Divide.

    We ask you to consider the Digital Divide because it is an incredibly relevant issue that affects nearly all nations in varying ways. With the development and widespread use of new technology, there is a growing gap between those with access to Information and Communication Technologies, and those without. Still, technology is being used across the globe to unite individuals with access to the internet, as we saw in the Arab Spring. How does the growth of technology impact people with and without access, and what should be done to address the varying issues around it.

    We are looking forward to a committee filled with creative and intellectual ideas on how nations should the Digital Divide, while encouraging technological development. Please read the brief to find important background information on our topic. See you at EBMUNC!
